Sapien Constitution v0.1

Preamble​

As the problems we face as a species rise to existential proportions it has become clear that our leaders and our institutions have failed us and that we must all pull together to address the problems that threaten the lives and livelihoods of each of us and our children.

Many of the problems we face affect many nations and are beyond the ability of any one nation to address. Such problems require coordination across national borders and boundaries.


The complexity of our global economy has risen beyond the ability of our representatives to understand or regulate. In order to effectively direct and govern our economy we must recognize that with great power comes great responsibility and that all participants, individual or institutional, have a responsibility toward the public good. 


The existing arrangement of our governments and economies have led to increasing wealth disparity and thus increasing disenfranchisement of the working class. We must recognize that those Sapiens who work tirelessly to support our society deserve direct representation in its governance.


Our governments have left our primary source of public funding, our currencies to be controlled by private institutions who have squandered public resources on enrichment of the few.


Our governing bodies are unable to keep pace with exponential technologies, and are prone to political manipulation by corporations in an unsustainable race to the bottom. 


Relentless gerrymandering has destroyed the ability of our governments to faithfully represent the will of our communities.


Our institutions have been captured by the interests of the few and no longer adequately function to serve the will of the people.

It is clear that in order to address these issues we must form a new nation, a network state that:

is a sovereign that has the social, economic, political, and legal apparatus to act in a united manner and cooperate internationally.


represents the will of all citizens regardless of national origin and recognizes our collective responsibility to address problems that affect us all.


equips its citizens with the tools for democratic governance, group sensemaking, and collective action. 


prioritizes the continued survival and flourishing of our species.


recognizes that public companies have a responsibility for the public good that supersedes profit incentives and holds such companies accountable to this responsibility.


recognizes the sovereignty of our communities and does its best to adequately represent the will of those communities.


prioritizes the continued survival and flourishing of our species.


ensures public funds are put toward the benefit of the public above private interests.

We therefore present the following proposal for the governance of the Sapien Nation.

Article 1: Parliament​

NEVER DOUBT THAT A SMALL GROUP OF THOUGHTFUL, COMMITTED CITIZENS CAN CHANGE THE WORLD; INDEED, IT’S THE ONLY THING THAT EVER HAS. — MEAD

Section 1: Overview​

All legislative power is vested in the Parliament of the Sapien Nation, which consists of the Hall of Ambassadors and the Hall of Sapiens.

Section 2: The Hall of Sapiens​

The Hall of Sapiens is composed of all Sapiens, people who hold veSPN (vote-escrowed) tokens.

No person will be discriminated against participation in the Hall of Sapiens because of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, psuedonmity, or national origin.

The Hall of Sapiens will choose their Speaker and other Officers; and will have the sole power of impeachment.

Section 3: The Hall of Ambassadors​

The Hall of Ambassadors is composed of members from DAOs that have signed a Sapien Nation Passport. Each DAO will receive three ambassador seats in the Hall of Ambassadors. Each DAO will have the autonomy to elect and remove ambassadors as they see fit. Each Ambassador will have one vote in the Hall of Ambassadors.

No person except a citizen of the Sapien Nation, as signified by their digital passport, will be eligible for the Hall of Ambassadors; no person will be discriminated against consideration for Ambassador because of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, psuedonmity, or national origin.

The Executive Council of the Sapien Nation will have no vote in the Hall of Ambassadors, unless they are equally divided.

The Ambassadors will choose their other Officers; and will have the sole power to try all impeachments. When sitting for that purpose, they will be on oath or affirmation. When a member of the Executive Council of the Sapien Nation is tried, the Chief Justice will preside: And no person will be convicted without the concurrence of two-thirds of the Ambassadors present.

Judgment in cases of impeachment will not extend further than to removal from the Executive Council, and disqualification to hold and enjoy any office of honor, trust or profit under the Sapien Nation: but the party convicted will nevertheless be liable and subject to indictment, trial, judgment and punishment, according to law.

Section 4: Elections​

The times, places and manner of holding elections for Ambassadors, will be decided by each DAO; but the Parliament may at any time by law make or alter such regulations. The Parliament will assemble at least once in every year, and such meeting will be on the first Monday in December unless they will by law appoint a different day.

Section 5: Procedure​

Each Hall will be the judge of the elections, returns and qualifications of its own members, and a majority of each will constitute a quorum to do business; but a smaller number may adjourn from day to day, and may be authorized to compel the attendance of absent members, in such manner, and under such penalties as each Hall may provide.

The Hall of Ambassadors may determine the rules of its proceedings, punish its members for disorderly behavior, and, with the concurrence of two thirds, expel a DAO.

Each Hall will keep a ledger of its proceedings using cryptographic signatures on a tamper-resistant public blockchain, and from time to time publish the same, excepting such parts as may in their judgment require secrecy; and the Yeas and Nays of the members of either Hall on any question will, at the desire of one fifth of those present, be entered on the ledger.

Neither Hall, during the session of Parliament, will, without the consent of the other, adjourn for more than three days.

Section 6: Compensation​

The Ambassadors and Sapiens will receive compensation for their services, to be ascertained by law, and paid out of the Treasury of the Sapien Nation. They will in all cases, except treason, felony and breach of the peace, be privileged from arrest during their attendance at the session of their respective Halls, and in going to and returning from the same; and for any speech or debate in either Hall, they will not be questioned in any other place.

No Ambassador, during the time for which they were elected, be appointed to any civil Office under the authority of the Sapien Nation, which will have been created, or compensation which will have been increased during such time; and no person holding any Office under the Sapien Nation, will be a member of either Hall during their continuance in Office.

Section 7: Bills​

All bills for raising revenue will originate in the Hall of Sapiens; but the Ambassadors may propose or concur with amendments as on other bills.

Every bill which will have passed the Hall of Sapiens and Ambassadors, will, before it becomes a law, be presented to the Executive Council of the Sapien Nation; if they approve they will sign it cryptographically, but if not they will return it, with their objections to that Hall in which it will have originated, who will enter the objections at large on their ledger, and proceed to reconsider it. If after such reconsideration two thirds of that Hall will agree to pass the bill, it will be sent, together with the objections, to the other Hall, by which it will likewise be reconsidered, and if approved by two thirds of that Hall, it will become a law. But in all such cases the votes of both Halls will be determined by Yeas and Nays, and the names of the persons voting for and against the bill will be entered on the ledger of each Hall respectively. If any bill will not be returned by the the Executive Council within ten Days (Sundays excepted) after it will have been presented to them, the same will be a law, in like manner as if they had signed it, unless the Parliament by their adjournment prevent its return, in which case it will not be a law.

Every order, resolution, or vote to which the concurrence of the Ambassadors and Hall of Sapiens may be necessary (except on a question of adjournment) will be presented to the Executive Council of the Sapien Nation; and before the same will take effect, will be approved by them, or being disapproved by them, will be repassed by two thirds of the Ambassadors and Hall of Sapiens, according to the rules and limitations prescribed in the case of a bill.

Section 8: Powers​

The Parliament will have power to lay and collect taxes, duties, imposts and excises, to pay the debts and provide for the common defense and general welfare of the Sapien Nation; but all duties, imposts and excises will be uniform throughout the Sapien Nation;

To borrow money on the credit of the Sapien Nation;

To regulate commerce with foreign nations, and among the several DAOs, and with all other network states;

To establish an uniform rule of naturalization, and uniform laws on the subject of bankruptcies throughout the Sapien Nation;

To mint tokens, regulate the value thereof, and of foreign tokens, and fix the standard of weights and measures;

To provide for the punishment of counterfeiting the securities and current token of the Sapien Nation;

To promote the progress of science and useful arts, by securing for limited times to authors and inventors the exclusive right to their respective writings and discoveries;

To constitute Tribunals inferior to the Supreme Court;

To define and punish piracies and felonies committed on the Internet, and offenses against the law of nations;

To declare war, grant letters of Marque and Reprisal, and make rules concerning captures on land and water;

To raise and support armies, but no appropriation of money to that use will be for a longer term than two years;

To provide and maintain a Navy;

To make rules for the government and regulation of the land and naval forces;

To provide for calling forth the Militia to execute the Laws of the Union, suppress Insurrections and repel Invasions;

To provide for organizing, arming, and disciplining, the Militia, and for governing such Part of them as may be employed in the Service of the United States, reserving to the States respectively, the Appointment of the Officers, and the Authority of training the Militia according to the discipline prescribed by Congress;

To exercise exclusive Legislation in all Cases whatsoever, over such District (not exceeding ten Miles square) as may, by Cession of particular States, and the Acceptance of Congress, become the Seat of the Government of the United States, and to exercise like Authority over all Places purchased by the Consent of the Legislature of the State in which the Same will be, for the Erection of Forts, Magazines, Arsenals, dock-Yards, and other needful Buildings;—And

To make all laws which will be necessary and proper for carrying into execution the foregoing powers, and all other powers vested by this Constitution in the government of the Sapien Nation, or in any Department or Officer thereof.

Section 9: Restrictions on Parliament​

The Migration or Importation of such Persons as any of the States now existing will think proper to admit, will not be prohibited by the Congress prior to the Year one thousand eight hundred and eight, but a Tax or duty may be imposed on such Importation, not exceeding ten dollars for each Person.

The Privilege of the Writ of Habeas Corpus will not be suspended, unless when in Cases of Rebellion or Invasion the public Safety may require it.

No Bill of Attainder or ex post facto Law will be passed.

No capitation, or other direct, Tax will be laid,[unless in Proportion to the Census or Enumeration herein before directed to be taken.5

No Tax or Duty will be laid on Articles exported from any State.

No Preference will be given by any Regulation of Commerce or Revenue to the Ports of one State over those of another: nor will Vessels bound to, or from, one State, be obliged to enter, clear, or pay Duties in another.

No Money will be drawn from the Treasury, but in Consequence of Appropriations made by Law; and a regular Statement and Account of the Receipts and Expenditures of all public Money will be published from time to time.

No Title of Nobility will be granted by the United States: And no Person holding any Office of Profit or Trust under them, will, without the Consent of the Congress, accept of any present, Emolument, Office, or Title, of any kind whatever, from any King, Prince, or foreign State.

Section 10: Restrictions on DAOs​

No DAO in the Sapien Nation will enter into any treaty, alliance, or confederation; grant letters of Marque and Reprisal; coin Money; emit Bills of Credit; make any Thing but gold and silver Coin a Tender in Payment of Debts; pass any Bill of Attainder, ex post facto Law, or Law impairing the Obligation of Contracts, or grant any Title of Nobility.

No DAO will, without the consent of the Parliament, lay any Imposts or Duties on Imports or Exports, except what may be absolutely necessary for executing it’s inspection Laws: and the net Produce of all Duties and Imposts, laid by any State on Imports or Exports, will be for the Use of the Treasury of the United States; and all such Laws will be subject to the Revision and Controul of the Congress.

No DAO will, without the Consent of Congress, lay any Duty of Tonnage, keep Troops, or Ships of War in time of Peace, enter into any Agreement or Compact with another State, or with a foreign Power, or engage in War, unless actually invaded, or in such imminent Danger as will not admit of delay.

Article 2: Executive Council​

THE HUMAN HEART IS THE FIRST HOME OF DEMOCRACY. IT IS WHERE WE EMBRACE OUR QUESTIONS. CAN WE BE EQUITABLE? CAN WE BE GENEROUS? CAN WE LISTEN WITH OUR WHOLE BEINGS, NOT JUST OUR MINDS, AND OFFER OUR ATTENTION RATHER THAN OUR OPINIONS? AND DO WE HAVE ENOUGH RESOLVE IN OUR HEARTS TO ACT COURAGEOUSLY, RELENTLESSLY, WITHOUT GIVING UP--EVER--TRUSTING OUR FELLOW CITIZENS TO JOIN WITH US IN OUR DETERMINED PURSUIT OF A LIVING DEMOCRACY? — WILLIAMS

Section 1: Overview​

All executive power is vested in the council of the Sapien Nation, which consists of three members that form a triarchy. The council is selected for the same term and will hold office for four years. The council will be elected as follows: one by the Hall of Sapiens, one by the Hall of Ambassadors, and one by the council itself.

The Hall of Sapiens will hold a direct quadratic vote in the form of cryptographic signatures on a tamper-resistant public blockchain. Each individual will be given “credits” equivalent to the amount of tokens they have staked. Credits will be allocated to choices and the counted votes will be the square root of credits allocated to each choice. The Hall of Sapiens will have the power to implement and enforce sybil resistance and conduct a fair and orderly election.

The Hall of Ambassadors will hold a vote in the form of cryptographic signatures on a tamper-resistant public blockchain. Each DAO will submit their vote, which will be the majority vote of the three Ambassadors representing that DAO. The Hall of Ambassadors will have the power to conduct a fair and orderly election.

The Executive Council will unanimously cast their vote in the form of cryptographic signatures on a tamper-resistant public blockchain.

Thus the next Executive Council is formed by the current council voting one person, the Hall of Ambassadors voting one person, and the Hall of Sapiens voting one person. If the current council cannot reach consensus within 60 days of the start of the election, the runner up of the Hall of Sapiens election is elected to the next Executive Council. If the Hall of Sapiens fails to hold a timely election or an election has an equal number of votes, then the Hall of Ambassadors hold another election or will cast a vote among the tied candidates, respectively. If the Hall of Ambassadors fails to hold a timely election or an election has an equal number of votes, then the current Executive Council will vote on a new candidate or cast a vote among the tied candidates, respectively.

The Speaker of the Hall, in the presence of the Hall of Ambassadors and Hall of Sapiens, will validate the election and confirm the results.

The Hall of Ambassadors may determine the day on which election begins; which will be the same throughout the world. No person except a citizen of the Sapien Nation, as signified by their signed digital passport, will be eligible for the Executive Council; no person will be discriminated against consideration for Executive Council because of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, psuedonmity, or national origin.

No person will be elected to the Executive Council more than twice, and no person who has held the office of Council, or acted as Council, for more than two years of a term to which some other person was elected Council will be elected to the office of the Council more than once.

In case of the removal of a member from the Executive Council, or a death, resignation, or inability to discharge the powers and duties of the council, the Hall of Ambassadors may by law provide for the case of Removal, death, resignation or inability, declaring what officer will then act as Executive Council, and such officer will act accordingly, until the disability be removed, or the next Executive Council will be elected.

The Executive Council will, at stated times, receive for their services, a compensation, which will neither be increased nor diminished during the period for which they have been elected, and they will not receive within that period any other compensation from the Sapien Nation, or any constituent DAOs. Before they enter the Executive Council, each member will take the following Oath or Affirmation: “I do solemnly swear (or affirm) that I will faithfully execute the Council of the Sapien Nation, and will to the best of my ability, preserve, protect and defend the Constitution of the Sapien Nation.”

Section 2: Powers​

The President will be Commander in Chief of the Army and Navy of the United States, and of the Militia of the several States, when called into the actual Service of the United States; he may require the Opinion, in writing, of the principal Officer in each of the executive Departments, upon any Subject relating to the Duties of their respective Offices, and he will have Power to grant Reprieves and Pardons for Offenses against the United States, except in Cases of Impeachment.

He will have Power, by and with the Advice and Consent of the Senate, to make Treaties, provided two thirds of the Senators present concur; and he will nominate, and by and with the Advice and Consent of the Senate, will appoint Ambassadors, other public Ministers and Consuls, Judges of the Supreme Court, and all other Officers of the United States, whose Appointments are not herein other- wise provided for, and which will be established by Law: but the Congress may by Law vest the Appointment of such inferior Officers, as they think proper, in the President alone, in the Courts of Law, or in the Heads of Departments. The President will have Power to fill up all Vacancies that may happen during the Recess of the Senate, by granting Commissions which will expire at the End of their next Session.

Section 3: State of the Network​

The Executive Council will from time to time give to the Parliament information of the State of the Network, and recommend to their consideration such measures as they will judge necessary and expedient; they may, on extraordinary occasions, convene both halls, or either of them, and in case of disagreement between them, with respect to the time of adjournment, the council may adjourn them to such time as the council will think proper; they will receive delegates and other public ministers; they will take care that the laws be faithfully executed, and will commission all the Officers of the Sapien Nation.

Section 4: Impeachment​

The Executive Council and all civil Officers of the Sapien Nation, will be removed from office on impeachment for, and conviction of, treason, bribery, or other high crimes and misdemeanors.

Article 3: Judicial​

YOU WILL STUDY THE WISDOM OF THE PAST, FOR IN A WILDERNESS OF CONFLICTING COUNSELS, A TRAIL HAS THERE BEEN BLAZED. YOU WILL STUDY THE LIFE OF MANKIND, FOR THIS IS THE LIFE YOU MUST ORDER, AND, TO ORDER WITH WISDOM, MUST KNOW. YOU WILL STUDY THE PRECEPTS OF JUSTICE, FOR THESE ARE THE TRUTHS THAT THROUGH YOU SHALL COME TO THEIR HOUR OF TRIUMPH. HERE IS THE HIGH EMPRISE, THE FINE ENDEAVOR, THE SPLENDID POSSIBILITY OF ACHIEVEMENT, TO WHICH I SUMMON YOU AND BID YOU WELCOME. _— _CARDOZO

Section 1: Overview​

The judicial power of the Sapien Nation will be vested in one Supreme Court and in such inferior courts as the Parliament may from time to time ordain and establish. The judges, both of the supreme and inferior courts, will hold their offices during good behavior, and will, at stated times, receive for their Services, a compensation, which will not be diminished during their continuance in office.

Section 2: Dispute Resolution​

The judicial power will extend to all cases, in law and equity, arising under this Constitution, the laws of the Sapien Nation, and treaties made, or which will be made, under their authority;— to all cases affecting Ambassadors, other public ministers and consuls;— to all cases of admiralty and maritime jurisdiction;— to controversies to which the Sapien Nation will be a party;— to controversies between two or more DAOs;— between a DAO and citizens of another DAO; between citizens of different DAOs;— between citizens of the same DAO claiming Lands under Grants of different States and between a DAO, or the citizens thereof;— and foreign DAOs, citizens or subjects.

In all cases affecting Ambassadors, other public ministers and consuls, and those in which a DAO will be party, the Supreme Court will have original jurisdiction. In all other cases before mentioned, the Supreme Court will have appellate jurisdiction, both as to law and fact, with such exceptions, and under such regulations as the Parliament will make.

The trial of all crimes, except in cases of impeachment, will be by jury; and such trial will be held in the DAO where the said crimes will have been committed; but when not committed within any DAO, the trial will be at such place or places as the Parliament may by law have directed.

Section 3: Treason​

Treason against the Sapien Nation, will consist only in levying War against them, or in adhering to their enemies, giving them aid and comfort. No person will be convicted of treason unless on the testimony of two witnesses to the same overt act, or on confession in open court.

The Parliament will have power to declare the punishment of treason, but no attainder of treason will work corruption of blood, or forfeiture except during the life of the person attainted.

Article 4: Inter-DAO​

Section 1: Overview​

Full faith and credit will be given in each DAO to the public acts, records, and judicial proceedings of every other DAO. And the Parliament may by general laws prescribe the manner in which such acts, records and proceedings will be proved, and the effect thereof.

Section 2: Citizen Rights and Extradition​

The citizens of each DAO will be entitled to all privileges and immunities of citizens in the several DAOs. A person charged in any DAO with treason, felony, or other crime, who will flee from justice, and be found in another DAO, will on demand of the executive authority of the DAO from which they fled, be delivered up, to be removed to the DAO having jurisdiction of the crime.

Section 3: New DAOs​

New DAOs, with a signed Sapien Nation passport, may be admitted by the Parliament into the Sapien Nation; but no new DAO will be formed or erected within the Jurisdiction of any other State; nor any State be formed by the Junction of two or more States, or Parts of States, without the Consent of the Legislatures of the States concerned as well as of the Congress.

The Congress will have Power to dispose of and make all needful Rules and Regulations respecting the Territory or other Property belonging to the United States; and nothing in this Constitution will be so construed as to Prejudice any Claims of the United States, or of any particular State.

Section 4: Protecting DAOs​

The Sapien Nation shall guarantee to every State in this Union a Republican Form of Government, will protect each DAO against invasion; and on application of the legislature, or of the executive (when the legislature cannot be convened) against domestic violence.

Article 5: Amendments​

The Parliament, whenever two thirds of both Halls will deem it necessary, will propose amendments to this Constitution, or, on the application of the legislatures of two thirds of the several DAOs, will call a convention for proposing amendments, which, in either Case, will be valid to all intents and purposes, as part of this Constitution, when ratified by the legislatures of three fourths of the several DAOs, or by conventions in three fourths thereof, as the one or the other mode of ratification may be proposed by the Parliament; provided that no amendment which may be made prior to the Year One thousand eight hundred and eight will in any manner affect the first and fourth clauses in the ninth Section of the first Article; and that no DAO, without its consent, will be deprived of its equal suffrage in the Hall of Ambassadors.

Article 6: Supremacy Clause​

This Constitution, and the laws of the Sapien Nation which will be made in pursuance thereof; and all treaties made, or which will be made, under the authority of the Sapien Nation, will be the supreme law of the land; and the judges in every DAO will be bound thereby, any thing in the Constitution or laws of any state to the contrary notwithstanding.

The Ambassadors and Sapiens before mentioned, and the members of the several DAO legislatures, and all executive and judicial Officers, both of the Sapien Nation and of the several DAOs, will be bound by oath or affirmation, to support this Constitution; but no religious test will ever be required as a qualification to any office or public trust under the Sapien Nation.

Article 7: Ratification​

The ratification of the conventions of three DAOs, will be sufficient for the establishment of this Constitution between the states so ratifying the same.

Amendments​

First: The Right to Sovereignty​

Sovereignty is the manifestation of the will of an individual or DAO. The DAO, as a group of people, has the right to personhood including the right to autonomy, existence, and self-determination. The Sovereignty of the DAO derives from the Sovereignty of its members and each individual has the right to choose the DAOs among which their Sovereignty is divided. No DAO, individual, or entity may interfere, restrict or impose costs on the Sovereignty of another DAO or individual.

Second: Freedom of Information​

Information is power. The ability to freely share information, whether through speech or expression in any medium, is vital to our ability to understand and engage with the world and each other. Each individual or DAO has the right to freely share information with others or choose not to share any information. No DAO, individual, or entity may compel anyone to share any information against their will.

Third: The Right to Economic Freedom​

Currency is the tool that directs our energy. Every individual and DAO has the right to choose how they direct their energy and resources including the right to not expend energy or resources. Every individual and DAO has the right to issue and govern their own currency so they may direct their energy and resources to the best of their ability. No DAO, individual, or entity may compel anyone to expend energy or resources against their will.
